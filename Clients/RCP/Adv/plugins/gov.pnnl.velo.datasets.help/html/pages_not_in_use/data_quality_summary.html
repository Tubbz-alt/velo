Data Quality Summary

Data quality can have many meanings depending on the context in which the term is used. For the purposes here, data quality is characterized by its handling after being produced. For the user, it is a measure of the fit of the data’s intended use in future scientific research. One or more descriptions can be chosen that best reflect the resource:

  • Raw data without quality control.
  • Raw data with quality control performed.
  • Data has been through a peer-review process, but not presented in a publication.
  • Data was used in a peer-reviewed publication.
  • Data was used in a non-peer-reviewed publication.
