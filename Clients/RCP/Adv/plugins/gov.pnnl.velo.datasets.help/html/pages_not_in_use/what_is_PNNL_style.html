What is PNNL style?

PNNL style refers to the citation format of references in your document and your document's bibliography. This style is based on a style amenable to a research laboratory and compatible with PNNL's internal information database, reference management software tools, and nationally accepted standards.

Guidelines for using the PNNL style can be found in the PNNL's Author's Guide.

Unfortunately, standards for dataset citation have not been approved by any governing bodies. Until then, current style guides can be used to craft an adequate citation.

PNNL style follows the Chicago Manual of Style. A suitable format for citing a dataset would be:

Author. Year submitted. Title of dataset. Version. Geographical place of production: Name of producing organization. [Distributed by] Location of distributor: Name of distributor. DOI.

Example:

McGrail P. 2013. R245fa Adsorption on MIL-101. Richland, WA: Pacific Northwest National Laboratory. doi: 10.15121/1148803

In this example, the producer and distributor are the same.
