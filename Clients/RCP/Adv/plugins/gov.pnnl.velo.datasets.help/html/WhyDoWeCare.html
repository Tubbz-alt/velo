Why do we care about dataset management?

Data management is the process of controlling the information generated during a research project at all stages of the data lifecycle, including capture, analysis, sharing and preservation. Funding agencies are requiring scientists to plan and execute good data management practices.Managing data became an integral part of the research process and public access to research federally funded is now a priority.

On 28 July 2014, the US Department of Energy (DOE) Office of Science announced new requirements for the management of digital research data. The new requirement outlined in a Dear Colleague letter from Patricia Dehmer, acting director of DOE's Office of Science, are in response to a White House Office of Science and Technology Policy directive.

Since October 1, 2014, all proposals submitted to the Office of Science for research funding are now required to include a data management plan (DMP) that describes whether and how the digital research data generated through the course of the proposed research will be shared and preserved.
